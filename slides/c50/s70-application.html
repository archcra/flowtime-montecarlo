Application - Computational biology 

Monte Carlo methods are used in various fields of computational biology, for example for Bayesian inference in
phylogeny, or for studying biological systems such as genomes, proteins, or membranes. The systems can
be studied in the coarse-grained or ab initio frameworks depending on the desired accuracy. Computer simulations
allow us to monitor the local environment of a particular molecule to see if some chemical reaction is happening
for instance. In cases where it is not feasible to conduct a physical experiment, thought experiments can be
conducted (for instance: breaking bonds, introducing impurities at specific sites, changing the local/global
structure, or introducing external fields).
